# Potion Profits

### About:
Potion Profits is a game where the main player is tasked with running a potion shop. Throughout the core game loop, the player is tasked with running, managing, brewing, and stocking their store for customer purchases. Facing rounds of perusing customers, they must sell their inventory and produce a daily profit. During any down time, the shop-owner can invest currency into new equipment or refilling stock to maintain an active customer base. In an effort to faster scale the profitability of their store, the player may also gamble at the local casino for a chance to quickly turn their hard-earned coins into prizes redeemed with casino chips. The player must carefully manage their time to ensure maximum profits and reconcile any debts with the local casino mogul.

Potion Profits is developed by CEJJO Studios and is available for download or in-browser play on [itch.io](https://potion-profits.itch.io/potion-profits)

### Motivation and Things We Learned:
This game was developed as our senior project under the guidance of Lucas Pierce. Thanks to Lucas, our group was able to pursue a project in an area of computing that none of us were relatively familiar with but that we were very excited to explore. Each of us learned the ins and outs of programming, concurrency, scene composition, and system development within the Godot game engine. Most of us learned how to draw pixel art for the many sprites, tilesets, and GUI elements present in Potion Profits. Most importantly we gained a stronger understanding of what it means to work as a team in a mostly-software oriented project. We dedicated time weekly to work in "game jams" and go over PRs as a group to ensure each feature was understood by the dev team. As we conclude this project, we leave Potion Profits in a playable state, and while we are uncertain of whether development will continue to the point of distribution, this project has inspired us to continue our own game-development journeys as a group.

Once again, we want to thank Lucas Pierce for advising us through this journey and giving us the opportunity to work as a group on this project.
